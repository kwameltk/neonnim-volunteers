VOLUNTEER HOUSE

Running Water

Fortunate are we to enjoy the convenience of running water in our volunteer accommodation, a luxury not commonly found in the local vicinity. This running water facilitates various needs, including the bathrooms, kitchen, and provides accessibility for your washing requirements.

Shared Bedrooms

Within the volunteer housing, bedrooms are thoughtfully arranged by gender, typically accommodating two bunk beds in each room. Essential storage needs are met through the provision of small shelving units, and ample space is available in the bedrooms to neatly store your suitcases and additional luggage.

Security

Ensuring the utmost security, the volunteer host family house is securely gated, providing a protective environment for volunteers. Belongings are safely kept indoors, adding an extra layer of assurance. Reviews from previous volunteers consistently highlight the reliable and commendable level of safety maintained within the house.

Kitchen

Within the house, a modest kitchen is at your disposal for preparing and cooking meals. While the provided items are fundamental, local shops offer a diverse array of kitchenware if you desire additional options. Included are a gas hob, pans, microwave, and essential cutlery and crockery, with the gas utility bill covered to alleviate your focus on food costs.

Bathrooms

The bathrooms within the housing are communal and shared among the volunteers and bedrooms. Equipped with modern flushing toilets, this, coupled with the availability of running water, is a notable luxury in our local area. Typically, even houses fortunate enough to have water closets often require manual flushing using a bucket fetched from an external storage tank.

Housekeeping

Residing in communal volunteer housing can present challenges as individuals may have varying living styles. Volunteers are entrusted with maintaining the accommodation, and while we recommend establishing a rotating schedule for routine cleaning tasks, it's important for volunteers to foster a spirit of teamwork and make compromises when necessary.There is also the option of paying someone to help with cleaning.

Mosquito Netting

Netting frames are installed on all house windows, providing a measure of protection. For added security, you might consider bringing a mosquito net to hang around your bed.

Volunteer Stay Options

At Nea Onnim Volunteers, we offer two distinct accommodation options to suit the preferences of our volunteers. The first option involves staying with a local host family, where volunteers have the unique opportunity to immerse themselves in Ghanaian culture and hospitality, under the guidance of our founder, Kwame.

This option fosters a close-knit community atmosphere and provides firsthand insight into daily life in Ghana. Alternatively, volunteers may choose to stay in our dedicated volunteer house, offering a more independent and private living environment. Both accommodation options feature personal kitchens, allowing volunteers to prepare meals at their convenience, as part of our self-catering program. Whichever option you choose, rest assured that your comfort and needs are our top priority at Nea Onnim Volunteers.
